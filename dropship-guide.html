Complete Beginner Workflow · Dropshipping 2026

Start Your Store.
Exact Steps. No Fluff.

Follow this in order. Don't skip ahead. You can be posting videos and taking orders within 48 hours.

STEP 01 Create Your Accounts DO THIS FIRST

Get everything set up before touching products. Don't skip any of these — you'll need them all.

  1. Shopify — go to shopify.com → sign up for the $1/month trial (3 months)
  2. Zendrop — go to zendrop.com → sign up free
  3. DSers — go to dsers.com → sign up free (backup supplier tool)
  4. CapCut — download the app on your phone
  5. TikTok — create a brand new account just for your store
  6. Claude — keep claude.ai open in your browser the whole time
  7. Shopify Payments — inside Shopify go to Settings → Payments → set up Shopify Payments or PayPal. Do this now or you can't receive money.
Note TikTok requires 1,000 followers before you can add a clickable link in your bio. Build that first. In the meantime, put your store URL in your first pinned comment on every video.
STEP 02 Find a Product 30–60 MIN

You're looking for a product that's already proven — something people are buying because they saw it on TikTok.

  1. Open TikTok and search: "TikTok made me buy it" or "[niche] gadget" (e.g. "car gadget", "kitchen gadget")
  2. Scroll and watch for the same product appearing across multiple different creators' videos
  3. Look for videos with 100k+ views — that's your proof of demand
  4. When you find a candidate: save 3–5 supplier videos from AliExpress or Zendrop directly (not other people's TikToks — more on this below)
  5. Quick gut check: if you can't instantly understand what the product does and why someone would want it → skip it and find another
⚠ Important — Don't Screen Record Other People's TikToks Reposting another creator's video violates TikTok's Terms of Service and can get your account banned before you've made a single sale. Instead: get video clips directly from your supplier on AliExpress or Zendrop, or use CapCut's free stock footage library. Same end result, no risk.
STEP 03 Import Product to Your Store 20–30 MIN

Try Zendrop first — it's faster and ships quicker. Use DSers as a backup if you can't find the product.

  1. Zendrop (try first): search the product → click "Import to Store" → connect to Shopify if prompted
  2. DSers (backup): find product on AliExpress through DSers → click "Add to DSers" → push to Shopify
  3. After importing, check the product is live in your Shopify Products tab
Supplier Stock Check Occasionally check your supplier's stock level. If they run out and you keep selling, you'll have angry customers and refund requests. Set a reminder to check weekly.
STEP 04 Build Your Product Page 1–2 HOURS

Inside Shopify → Products → click your imported product. Edit each section below.

— TITLE —

Keep it clean and descriptive. No emojis, no gimmicks.

Example: "Portable Car Cleaning Gel"
— DESCRIPTION —

Open Claude and paste this prompt:

Write a high-converting product page for [product name]. Include: - A strong opening hook (1–2 sentences) - 3–5 benefit-focused bullet points - A short "why you need this" paragraph - A 3-question FAQ Keep the tone confident but not salesy. Plain language only.

Copy the result straight into Shopify.

— IMAGES —
  1. Use supplier images from Zendrop or DSers
  2. Delete any blurry, watermarked, or low-quality images
  3. Keep 3–6 clean images max
— SETTINGS —
  1. Turn Track Quantity OFF (dropshipping doesn't need it)
  2. Set shipping to Free Shipping
— STORE SETUP —
  1. Go to Online Store → Themes → keep the default Dawn theme
  2. Click Customize → add your product image and a "Buy Now" button to the homepage. Keep it simple.
  3. Go to Settings → Policies → click "Generate templates" → save all
STEP 05 Create Your Videos 1–2 HOURS DAILY

Use supplier video clips from AliExpress or Zendrop — they're made for this. Edit them in CapCut to make them feel fresh and original.

  1. Download 3–5 product video clips directly from your supplier listing
  2. Open CapCut → import your clips
  3. Cut any branding, logos, or supplier watermarks
  4. Rearrange clips, add a zoom effect every 1–2 seconds to keep attention
  5. Add big bold captions (CapCut auto-captions work well)
  6. Add a trending audio track from CapCut or TikTok sounds
— THE HOOK —

The first 2 seconds either keep someone watching or lose them. Open Claude and use this:

Rewrite this TikTok hook to stop someone mid-scroll. Make it punchy, specific, and under 8 words: [paste your current hook here]

Replace your opening text in the video with the new hook.

Ideal video length 7–15 seconds. Short enough to rewatch, long enough to show the product working.
STEP 06 Post Your Videos DAILY
  1. Upload to TikTok first (highest organic reach)
  2. Then cross-post to Instagram Reels and Facebook Reels
  3. Post 3–5 videos per day, spread out: morning / afternoon / evening
  4. Caption: keep it short and curious — "You didn't know you needed this" / "This changed everything"
  5. Reply to every comment for the first hour after posting — it signals engagement to the algorithm
Store link in bio TikTok locks the link-in-bio until 1,000 followers. Until then: paste your URL in the first comment of every video and tell people "link in comments."
STEP 07 Your Daily Routine REPEAT EVERY DAY

This is the whole job until you get sales. Simple and repeatable.

  1. Download 3–5 fresh supplier video clips
  2. Edit into 3–5 videos in CapCut
  3. Use Claude to write hooks and captions
  4. Post all videos spaced out through the day
  5. Reply to every comment
  6. Check Shopify for orders
STEP 08 When You Get a Sale WITHIN 24 HRS
  1. Go to Shopify → Orders → click the order
  2. Open Zendrop or DSers → find the order → click "Order Product"
  3. Enter the customer's address and pay the supplier cost
  4. The supplier ships directly to your customer — you keep the margin
Fulfillment tip Fulfill orders within 24 hours. Customers expect fast communication even if shipping takes time. A quick "your order is confirmed and on its way" message goes a long way.
STEP 09 When a Video Takes Off — Scale It WHEN READY

Don't judge a video until 48 hours after posting — TikTok sometimes distributes slowly. If after 48 hours a video has 10k+ views, that's your signal.

  1. Identify what worked: the hook, the clip, the caption, the audio
  2. Make 5 more videos using the same structure
  3. Keep the same hook style but change the exact wording slightly each time
  4. Don't change the product yet — squeeze everything out of a winner first
When to switch products If you've posted 30+ videos over 2 weeks and nothing is getting traction, try a different product. Don't redesign the store — just swap the product.
STOP What to Ignore READ THIS

Most beginners fail because they do too much of the wrong things. Don't.

  • Do not redesign your store every few days
  • Do not wait until your videos feel "perfect"
  • Do not buy extra tools or courses before you've made your first sale
  • Do not run paid ads — you have no data yet, you'll just burn money
  • Do not repost other creators' TikToks — it risks your account
  • Do not give up after 3 days of no sales — this takes consistency

Your End State

If you follow this daily, within 2–4 weeks you'll have real data, real views, and your first sales. That's when it gets interesting.
